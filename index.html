OUR STORY

Andrew William Stoddart was born on February 20th, 2000 and died suddenly while playing soccer on 

Monday, May 11, 2015 in Kintore, Ontario. If there was an AED on hand Andrew might still be with us 

today. Andrew had no diagnosed heart condition, and was a very athletic 15-year-old boy. No one should 

have to loose a child, brother, sister, mother or any other family member because the right piece of 

medical equipment was not available to save them. 


Andrew Stoddart’s Legacy #11Forever

OUR VISION

Our vision is to have AED'S at all sports facilities, and public spaces across Canada, and have people 

around who are willing to perform CPR and use an AED. Help us to spread the word by sharing our story 

and liking our Facebook page. 


Donations are fully tax deductible and can be easily made on line, by clicking the link.


WE ARE DETERMINED TO MAKE ANDREW'S LEGACY A REALITY. WE CONTINUE TO FUNDRAISE AND PLACE AED’S AT VARIOUS FACILITIES ACROSS THE AREA.

THE FIRST 15 MINUTES ARE CRUCIAL FOR SURVIVAL. EVERY MINUTE THAT PASSES WITHOUT CPR AND DEFIBRILLATION THE CHANCES OF SURVIVAL DECREASE BY 7-10%
